Despite decades of deliberation, residential property tax is yet to be levied at a national scale in China. Recently, as the property market enters a persistent downturn and local governments face increasing fiscal pressure, property tax reform has become more pressing. Why has the central government not accelerated the adoption of the tax after initial pilot programs were established in 2011? Would the recent property market downturn incentivize local governments to seek the adoption of the tax?
